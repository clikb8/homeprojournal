Smart Homeowners in Your State Are Using These 9 Overlooked Savings Programs in 2026

By Margaret Ellison · Consumer Savings Desk  |  6 min read

Owning a home keeps getting more expensive — but a growing number of homeowners are quietly trimming the cost of major repairs, upgrades, and monthly bills. The common thread isn't a coupon. It's checking whether they qualify and comparing free, no-obligation quotes online before they ever pay out of pocket. Below are nine programs and services worth checking in your area while they're still available.

No. 1 — Roofing

A New Service Helps Homeowners Compare Roof Replacement Quotes

A roof over 15 years old — or one showing leaks or storm damage — can quietly drive up energy bills and lead to expensive water damage if it's ignored. A free matching service lets homeowners enter their ZIP code and compare quotes from screened local roofing contractors, with no obligation to hire anyone.

Homeowners generally qualify to compare quotes if:

  • You own your home
  • Your roof is roughly 15+ years old
  • You live in a participating ZIP code

No. 2 — Gutter Guards

Gutter Guards Can Spare Homeowners the Ladder — and the Water Damage

Clogged gutters can lead to roof leaks, foundation problems, and thousands of dollars in water damage. Clearing them the old way means climbing a ladder — a chore most homeowners would rather avoid. Gutter guards keep leaves and debris out while letting rainwater flow freely, and a free service can connect you with local installers to compare quotes.

Compare gutter guard quotes »

No. 3 — Home Equity

Homeowners in Your Area May Be Able to Borrow From Their Home Equity

With home values near record highs, many homeowners have built up significant equity without realizing how much. A home equity loan can let eligible owners access some of that value — to consolidate higher-interest debt or fund a major project — without touching their existing first-mortgage rate.

You may be able to check your options if:

  • You've owned your home for 2+ years
  • You have usable equity built up
  • Checking your options won't impact your credit score

No. 4 — Debt Relief

Debt Relief Programs for Homeowners Carrying $15,000 or More in Credit Card Debt

Rising costs and high interest rates have left many households carrying credit card balances that feel impossible to pay down. Debt relief programs may help eligible homeowners reduce what they owe and resolve it faster than minimum payments alone would allow.

These programs generally fit homeowners with $15,000 or more in unsecured debt. Checking whether you qualify is free and doesn't commit you to anything.

No. 5 — Home Warranty

A Home Warranty Can Help You Avoid Surprise Repair Bills

When an air conditioner, water heater, or major appliance gives out, the repair bill never seems to arrive at a good time — and standard homeowners insurance usually won't cover it. A home warranty plan can help cover the repair or replacement of covered systems and appliances, which makes costs far more predictable on a fixed monthly budget.

Plans and pricing vary, so it's worth comparing a few options for your area before deciding.

Compare home warranty plans »

No. 6 — Walk-In Tubs & Showers

Walk-In Tubs and Showers Are an Easy Way to Upgrade a Dated Bathroom

Updating a tired bathroom is one of the highest-impact home improvements you can make. Walk-in tubs and walk-in showers add low-step entry, non-slip surfaces, and a cleaner, more modern look — improving both safety and everyday comfort. Whether you want the comfort of a soaking tub or a simpler walk-in shower, it adds value and function to your home.

A quick eligibility check shows what styles and pricing are available from trusted local installers near you, at no cost and with no obligation.

No. 7 — Windows

Homeowners Are Comparing Energy-Efficient Window Quotes by ZIP Code

Older windows let heat escape in winter and seep in during summer, quietly pushing up energy bills month after month. Newer energy-efficient windows seal far better and can make a home more comfortable year-round. This free service helps homeowners see whether they qualify for a quote and connects them with local installers.

You may qualify for a free quote if:

  • You own your home
  • Your windows are 6+ years old
  • You live in a qualifying ZIP code

No. 8 — Siding

New Siding Can Cut Drafts and Refresh a Home's Exterior

Worn or aging siding doesn't just look tired — it can let in drafts and quietly push up heating and cooling costs. Modern siding improves insulation and instantly refreshes a home's curb appeal. This free service connects homeowners with local siding contractors for no-obligation quotes, so you can see what an update would cost before committing.

No. 9 — Home Insurance

Home Insurance Rates Have Jumped — Many Homeowners Are Overpaying Without Knowing It

Home insurance premiums have climbed sharply in recent years, and many homeowners are quietly paying far more than they need to — often because they haven't compared rates since they first bought their policy. Loyalty rarely gets rewarded here, and switching or re-shopping can mean real savings on the same coverage.
