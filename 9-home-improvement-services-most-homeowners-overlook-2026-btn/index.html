9 Home Services Most Homeowners Overlook in 2026

By Margaret Ellison · Home Improvement Desk  |  6 min read

Owning a home keeps getting more expensive — but a growing number of homeowners are taking a more deliberate approach to major repairs and upgrades. Instead of calling the first contractor they find, they're requesting free, no-obligation quotes online before they ever pay out of pocket. Below are nine home improvement services worth checking in your area.

No. 1 — Roofing

Homeowners Can Get Connected With Local Roofing Contractors

An aging roof — or one showing leaks or storm damage — can quietly drive up energy bills and lead to expensive water damage if it's ignored. A free service lets homeowners enter their ZIP code and be connected with screened local roofing contractors, with no obligation to hire anyone.

You may request a free quote if:

  • You own your home
  • You live in a participating ZIP code

No. 2 — Windows

Homeowners Can Receive Energy-Efficient Window Quotes by ZIP Code

Older windows let heat escape in winter and seep in during summer, quietly pushing up energy bills month after month. Newer energy-efficient windows seal far better and can make a home more comfortable year-round. This service helps homeowners receive a Free Quote and connects them with local installers.

You may request a free quote if:

  • You own your home
  • You live in a participating ZIP code

No. 3 — Home Equity

Homeowners in Your Area May Be Able to Borrow From Their Home Equity

With home values near record highs, many homeowners are sitting on far more equity than they realize. A home equity loan lets eligible owners put some of that value to work — consolidating higher-interest debt or funding a major project — without touching the low first-mortgage rate they already have. For a lot of homeowners, it's the difference between borrowing against the house and reaching for a credit card.

You may be able to check your options if:

  • You've owned your home for 2+ years
  • You have usable equity built up
  • Checking your options won't impact your credit score

No. 4 — Walk-In Tubs

Walk-In Tubs Are Helping Older Adults Stay Safe and Independent at Home

For many older homeowners, the bathroom is quietly the riskiest room in the house. A walk-in tub adds low-step entry, non-slip surfaces, and built-in seating — making bathing easier and helping reduce the risk of a slip. With the comfort of a deep soaking tub, it's less about luxury and more about staying comfortable and independent at home for longer.

Request a free quote to see the styles and pricing available from trusted local installers near you.

No. 5 — Home Warranty

A Home Warranty Can Help You Avoid Surprise Repair Bills

When an air conditioner quits in July or the water heater goes in the middle of winter, the bill never seems to land at a convenient time — and standard homeowners insurance usually won't touch it. A home warranty plan helps cover the repair or replacement of major systems and appliances, turning an unpredictable four-figure surprise into a manageable monthly cost.

Plans and pricing vary by provider, so comparing a few options for your area is worth a few minutes before you decide.

Compare home warranty plans »

No. 6 — Debt Relief

Debt Relief Programs for Homeowners Carrying $15,000 or More in Credit Card Debt

Rising costs and high interest rates have left many households — including retirees on fixed incomes — carrying credit card balances that feel impossible to pay down. Debt relief programs may help eligible homeowners cut what they owe and clear it far faster than minimum payments ever could.

These programs generally fit homeowners with $15,000 or more in unsecured debt. Checking whether you qualify is free, takes a couple of minutes, and commits you to nothing.

Check your eligibility »

No. 7 — Home Insurance

Many Homeowners Are Overpaying for Home Insurance Without Knowing It

Home insurance rates have climbed sharply in recent years, and many homeowners are still on a policy they set up years ago without ever shopping around. A few minutes comparing quotes can reveal meaningfully lower rates — often with the same or better coverage. This free service lets you compare home insurance options from multiple providers side by side, with no obligation to switch.

You may be able to compare rates if:

  • You own your home
  • Your current policy is up for renewal or you want a better rate
Compare Home Insurance Rates »

No. 8 — Walk-In Showers

Walk-In Showers Make Bathing Easier and Safer for Older Adults

A walk-in shower replaces the high step of a traditional tub with a low or barrier-free entry, adding non-slip flooring, grab bars, and room for a built-in seat. For homeowners who prefer a quick, simpler routine over a soak, it's a modern update that helps reduce the risk of a slip and keeps daily life easier at home.

Request a free quote and a local installer can show you the designs and pricing that fit your bathroom.

Request a Free Quote »

No. 9 — Auto Insurance

Homeowners Who Bundle Auto and Home Insurance Often Pay Less for Both

Most insurers offer a multi-policy discount when you bundle your home and auto coverage together — but many homeowners never ask about it. If your policies are with different providers, you could be leaving money on the table every year. Comparing bundled quotes takes a few minutes and can show you whether switching both policies to one provider makes sense for your situation.

You may be able to save if:

  • Your home and auto policies are with different providers
  • You haven't compared rates in the last 12 months
