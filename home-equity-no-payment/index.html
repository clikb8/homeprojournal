Need Cash but Can't Stomach Another Monthly Payment? Here's What Your State Homeowners Are Doing Instead

HP
HomeProJournal Staff | Home Finance Guide

Local Homeowners: Think about where they're at — the last thing most people want is a new monthly payment, or to refinance the low mortgage rate they fought to get. Yet life still happens: repairs, college, medical bills, debt that won't quit.

That's why more and more homeowners are stepping past loans entirely and accessing their equity a different way — one that doesn't add a single dollar to their monthly bills.

Key Takeaways
  • Access your equity with no monthly payment and no interest
  • Keep your existing mortgage — and the low rate that came with it
  • It's not a loan: you share a portion of your home's future change in value
  • Requires 620+ FICO, 35%+ equity, primary residence (no manufactured homes)
  • Free, no-obligation estimate in about 60 seconds

The Monthly Payment Trap

If you're strapped for cash today, you already know the cycle. A home equity loan, a HELOC, a cash-out refinance — they all hand you money and then hand you a new monthly payment with the added sting of today's rate environment. That payment can be on you for years.

Worse, a cash-out refinance can mean giving up the low mortgage rate you locked in years ago. For millions of homeowners, that alone makes borrowing a non-starter — why reset your entire mortgage at a higher rate just to access cash you already earned?

Many homeowners with sub-4% mortgages are bypassing cash-out refinancing entirely — choosing equity sharing to avoid resetting their mortgage at today's higher rates.

A Different Way to Use Your Equity

Home equity sharing flips the model. Instead of borrowing against your home, you access a portion of your equity as a lump sum of cash today. In exchange, you agree to share a portion of your home's change in value when you sell or at the end of the term.

The part that gets homeowners' attention: there are no monthly payments and no interest. Your existing mortgage and its rate stay exactly as they are. You simply unlock cash that's been sitting idle in your home.

No monthly payments. No interest. Keep your current mortgage and rate. Home equity sharing is not a loan — it's a way to access your equity in exchange for a share of your home's future value.

Is It Right for You?

  • You have meaningful equity but don't want a new monthly payment
  • You want to keep your existing (low) mortgage rate
  • You need cash for renovations, debt consolidation, or a safety net
  • You'd rather not deal with the expense and credit hassles of a refinance
  • Your income is irregular, retired, or self-employed — and lenders keep saying no

Eligibility and the amount you can access depends on your home, your equity, and your location. The fastest way to find out is to check your estimate — it takes about a minute and costs nothing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is home equity sharing a loan?

No. It's not a loan and it doesn't appear as debt on your credit report. You're selling a small stake in your home's future appreciation in exchange for a lump sum today. There's no APR, no monthly payment, and no interest charges.

Will I have a monthly payment or interest?

No. That's the core difference from a HELOC or cash-out refinance. With equity sharing, there is no monthly payment and no interest rate. You settle only when you sell, refinance on your own terms, or reach the end of the term.

Do I still own my home?

Yes, completely. You remain the full legal owner of your property. The company has a lien that gets settled at a future exit event, but you live in your home normally — your name is still on the deed.

How much equity can I access?

Investment amounts range from $30,000–$500,000, typically 5%–15% of your home's current market value. You'll need a 620+ FICO score, 35%+ equity, and a primary residence (no manufactured homes). Checking your estimate takes about a minute and is free.

What can I use the cash for?

Anything you choose — debt payoff, home renovations, medical bills, education, retirement income, starting a business. There are no restrictions on how you use the funds once they're wired to your account.

Are there any fees?

Yes. There is a one-time transaction fee — typically around 3.9% of the investment amount — deducted at closing. There are no monthly fees, no ongoing interest charges, and no prepayment penalties. When you settle (at sale or end of term), you repay the original investment amount plus or minus the agreed share of your home's change in value. Total cost depends on how your home's value changes over time.

Does checking my estimate cost anything or hurt my credit?

The estimate check is free. Many providers use a soft credit pull for the initial check — confirm the specific process with your provider, as practices vary. A hard inquiry typically only occurs if you choose to proceed with a full application.
